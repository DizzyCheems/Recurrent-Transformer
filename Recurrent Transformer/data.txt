What is the freezing point of water?
The freezing point of water is 0 degrees Celsius, or 32 degrees Fahrenheit. This is the critical temperature at which the liquid state of water transforms into solid ice. The freezing point of water holds significant importance as a fundamental physical constant, playing a crucial role in numerous scientific and everyday applications.
